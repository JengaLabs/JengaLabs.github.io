• Who Am I?

    There are three things extremely hard: steal, a diamond, and to know one’s self. - Benjamin Franklin
       I heard that quote in the middle of a Civilization VI game and took a moment to resonate with it. I struggle to accurately portray my own character to others in a concise manner.I prefer using my environment to build an image of me. Thus, here are questions from those closest to me to internet strangers.

  • What is your favorite animal and why?

    I chose the pig in elementary school age for its simplicity and my love of the game Minecraft. The answer and reasons remain the same.

  • Why did you choose your major?

    I’ll want to pursue a degree in Aerodynamics while in the Air Force so I can work in the space industry afterwards.

  • Why do you think you are drawn to the things you do?

    Growing up watching people complete huge projects in real life and in games is a big inspiration. Why my specific projects boils down to my love of creation.

  • What are your realistic future goals vs your dream future goals?

    My realistic future will be working in the Space Industry in a big city as a small cog in the big machine. My dream future is to be acting director of a NASA project as big as the Manhattan project. Leading an entire town towards one goal.

  • What kind of living situation would make you the happiest?

    I love living in a house I can modify to my liking. Wanna put a hole through the wall or paint the walls ect. However I don’t want a yard personally, rather a community garden of sorts. Another big plus would be not having to own a car. Walking and biking to places always feels great.

  • What niche interests do you have?

    While sewing isn’t considered niche, those I know wouldn’t consider it typical.

  • Are you satisfied with the direction of your life?

    Never thought I’d see myself joining the Military, getting married and having the hobbies I do. I wouldn’t have it anyother way.

  • What kind of books do you like to read? What’s your favorite book?

    I love self help books and not for the typical reasons. I enjoy reflecting on how I live my life compared to others. Looking for any improvements that could be made. As well getting a look at other peoples lives.

  • Explain what you understand as computer programming.

    Computer programming has always just been finding a clever way to make a computer perform a task.

  • If you can change one thing about yourself, what would it be?

    Jumping projects, I hate looking back at unfinished projects. I always intend to finish them but the spark just dies somewhere along the way. I’m trying to be better about it though.
